## Caveats
1. **MFE-only data**: We don't know if MAE happened before MFE. The trade might have stopped out BEFORE reaching the BE trigger. This is the biggest source of overestimation.
2. **No price path**: MFE tells us the max favorable move but not when it happened. A trade could spike +$2 for one tick then dump — the BE stop might not fill.
3. **Look-ahead bias**: The BE trigger thresholds are chosen after seeing the data.
4. **Slippage model is symmetric**: Real slippage on stops is often worse during fast moves.
5. **Commission rebate assumes 70% rate persists**: If the rebate program changes, the economics shift significantly.
